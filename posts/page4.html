151. How a ‘fish taxi’ helped lead to a record return of sockeye salmon near a Washington hydroelectric dam

Factor This™ | Mon, 13 Oct 2025 18:56:51 +0000

Sockeye populations in the Baker River system had declined to just 99 returning fish in the mid-1980s, bringing the species to near extinction in these waters near Mount Baker.

A 'fish taxi' program has significantly contributed to a record return of sockeye salmon near a hydroelectric dam in Washington. This initiative involves transporting fish safely past the dam, allowing for increased salmon populations in the region.
