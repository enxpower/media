151. Two-decade hydropower plunge at big Colorado River dams

Factor This™ | Mon, 03 Nov 2025 18:35:00 +0000

Hydropower output at Hoover and Glen Canyon dams has significantly declined since 2000 due to reduced water levels in Lakes Mead and Powell.

Hydropower generation at major dams along the Colorado River has decreased significantly over the past two decades, primarily due to prolonged drought conditions and climate change impacts. This decline poses challenges for water management and energy production in the region, affecting both local ecosystems and communities reliant on these resources.
