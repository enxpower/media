151. Solar growth cushions Colorado River hydropower declines

Factor This™ | Mon, 27 Oct 2025 17:24:00 +0000

Arizona water policy officials met in Phoenix to discuss extending a drought emergency due to low reservoirs and minimal precipitation.

Solar energy growth in the southwestern United States is helping to offset declines in hydropower production from the Colorado River. As climate change and prolonged drought conditions impact water levels, the increase in solar capacity is providing a crucial buffer for energy supply in the region.
