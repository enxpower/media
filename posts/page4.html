151. Two-decade hydropower plunge at big Colorado River dams

Factor This™ | Mon, 03 Nov 2025 18:35:00 +0000

Hydropower output at Hoover and Glen Canyon dams has significantly declined since 2000 due to reduced water levels in Lakes Mead and Powell.

Hydropower generation at major Colorado River dams has significantly declined over the past two decades, primarily due to prolonged drought conditions and climate change impacts. This reduction in water availability has led to lower reservoir levels, affecting the capacity to generate electricity from these hydropower facilities.
