151. Two-decade hydropower plunge at big Colorado River dams

Factor This™ | Mon, 03 Nov 2025 18:35:00 +0000

Hydropower output at Hoover and Glen Canyon dams has significantly declined since 2000 due to reduced water levels in Lakes Mead and Powell.

Hydropower generation at major dams on the Colorado River has seen a significant decline over the past two decades, primarily due to prolonged drought conditions and climate change impacts. This reduction in hydropower output poses challenges for energy supply and water management in the region.
